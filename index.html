Who We Are

A Community Built on Solidarity & Trust

Drumvale Riverview Welfare Group is a registered community organisation serving all households in Drumvale Riverview Estate, Kamulu, Machakos County. We exist to ensure no member faces life's hardest moments alone.

From bereavement support to collective land acquisition, bursary awards, and estate security, our work spans every dimension of community life. We are non-profit, non-partisan, and non-denominational.

📋 Welfare Bylaws (Terms & Conditions)

1. Registration & Eligibility

All members MUST register with a non-refundable registration fee of Ksh 100. New members must also pay an entrance fee of Ksh 2,000.

2. Welfare Coverage

Covers: Principal member; nuclear family (children up to age 30, spouse); biological parents of both spouses.

3. Contribution Amounts per Case

Ksh 300 (nuclear family member); Ksh 500 (principal member); Ksh 200 (parents/parents-in-law).

4. Approved Payout Figures

Parents & parents-in-law — Ksh 30,000 | Nuclear member — Ksh 60,000 | Principal member — Ksh 100,000.

5. Contribution Deadline

Strictly 7 days from announcement date. No exceptions.

6. Consistency Requirement

Members with arrears of three consecutive cases shall not qualify for any assistance.

7. New Member Waiting Period

New members wait TWO months from date of entry before welfare assistance applies.

Group Constitution

Drumvale Riverview Welfare Group — Kamulu, Machakos County

ARTICLE 1 — NAME AND LOCATION

The name of the group shall be DRUMVALE RIVERVIEW WELFARE GROUP. Registered under the Ministry of Labour and Social Protection of Kenya. Non-profit, non-partisan, and non-denominational.

ARTICLE 3 — OBJECTIVES

  • Provide welfare support during bereavement.
  • Foster savings and collective investment.
  • Promote education and award bursaries.
  • Enhance security and social cohesion.
  • Undertake community development projects.

ARTICLE 5 — FEES & CONTRIBUTIONS

Registration fee: Ksh 100 (once-off). Joining contribution: Ksh 2,000 (once-off). Monthly welfare contribution: Ksh 500.

ARTICLE 7 — GOVERNANCE

Elected Executive Committee: Chairperson, Vice-Chairperson, Secretary, Treasurer, and three Ordinary Members. Term: 2 years.

ARTICLE 9 — MEETINGS

AGM: February and September annually. Special General Meetings: 3 days' notice for urgent matters.

ARTICLE 21 — WELFARE BYLAWS

Contributions: Ksh 300 (nuclear) | Ksh 500 (principal) | Ksh 200 (parents). Payouts: Ksh 60,000 (nuclear) | Ksh 100,000 (principal) | Ksh 30,000 (parents). Deadline: 7 days from announcement. New members: 2-month waiting period.

ARTICLE 23 — LOANS

Loans from members' savings at 10% interest, 3-month term. Qualifies after 6 months of saving. Vetted by loan committee.

ARTICLE 25 — RESIGNATION

Members may resign immediately. Receive 80% of savings. Members with arrears in more than 3 cases are automatically removed from welfare register.
